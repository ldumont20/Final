Things To Do

There are so many things to do in Hew Hampshire. Below is a list of the most popular attractions.


The Flume Gorge


The Flume Gorge is one of the most beautiful places in New Hampshire to visit. It is a natural gorge that extends over 800 feet horizontally at the base of Mount Liberty. Enjoy a 2 mile self-guided nature walk that loops through the gorge. Start your journey by hiking down between two mountains. Along the way, get up close to a waterfall, walk parallel to a rushing river, enter a couple of caves, walk across covered bridges, and witness the amazing mountain view.


The Polar Caves


Polar Caves is a set of glacially-formed caves located in the White Mountains.The cave park contains boulder gardens, boardwalks, a small petting zoo, as well as multiple caves to maneuver through.


Muddy Paws Dog Sledding


Get up close and personal with the dogs at Muddy Paws Dog Sledding. Muddy Paws offers year-round dog sledding adventures that are fun for the entire family. First, start by getting to know the many rescue dogs. You then have the oppurtunity to help harness and hitch the dogs to the sled. The one hour musher-guided tour takes you around the grounds on both flat land and hills. The proceeds gained for tours even helps care for all the dogs at Muddy Paws. Muddy Paws Dog Sledding is experience like none other and one you will never forget.

Cannon Mountain Aerial Tramway


Take a tram hundreds of feet up the beautiful New Hampshire Mountains. The Cannon Mountain Aerial Tramway brings visitors in an 80 passenger cable car to the 4080 foot summit of Cannon Mountain in about eight minutes. On a clear day, you can see stunning views from New Hampshire, Maine, Vermont, New York, and parts of Canada. At the summit, you can find amazing walking and hiking paths, a cafe, and 360 degree observation deck. The tramway is a must-see, thrilling attraction that is perfect for the whole family.

Pemi Valley Moose Tours


Pemi Valley Moose Tours are seasonal, educational bus tours that offer up-close view of moose in forests and historical sites. The tours are usually around three and a half hours long and feature a guided tour of the area, a video about moose, and interesting historical sites along the way. The tours happen nightly and you have a 99% chance of seeing a moose while on the tour.

Hobo Railroad and Mini Golf


The Hobo Railroad brings you on a journey through the wooded forests of the White Mountains, twisting alongside the famous Pemi River. Your admission also includes a meal and scheduled entertainment on the train. The Railroad was even voted “Best Ride For Kids” by New Hampshire Magazine. When your journey is over, don’t forget to stop by the Hobo Mini Golf Course, as well.

Santa's Village


It feels like Christmas everyday in Santa's Village. Come ride rollercoasters and other amusement rides, plays games and scavenger hunts, visit different parts of the North Pole, meet real reindeer and elves, and see Santa CLause himself. Santa's Village is an attraction that's perfect for the whole family.

Whale's Tale Water Park


Whale's Tale Water Park is a 17 acre waterpark that's fit for the whole family. One has the option to ride the big waves or stay in the more gentle waters. SLide down speed slides, twist through flume slides, and splash down the 360 foot tall tube ride. Admission includes access to 11 waterslides, a wavepool, a 1/4 mile lazy river, and a kids play area. Tubes are even free of charge!

Lost River Gorge and Boulder Caves


Lost River Gorge is a place of incredible beauty created by weather and time. The shaping of the caves began 300 million years ago during the ice age when glaciers more than a mile thick lifted and desposited rocks that would later become huge mountains. Over time, the eroding of the rock formed what is known today as the Lost River Gorge. Lost River is named from the the brook draining from southeast part disappears below the surface of the gorge. The Gorge is filled with giant blocks of granite through which the brook cascades along, evenutally joining to the Pemigewasset River. Admission to Lost River includes a 3/4 mile boardwalk through the gorge. From there, you can choose to remain on the boardwalk take on the challenge of exploring the boulder caves. Also don't forget to try manuevering you way through the famous Lemon Squeezer Cave. Lost River Gorge is truly an adventure and experience you will never forget!
